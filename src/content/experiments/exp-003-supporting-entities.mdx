## Motivation

Entity SEO practice often asserts that surrounding entities reinforce a
canonical node. Public, falsifiable tests of that claim in AI Search contexts
are scarce.

### Target relationship under study

```text
George Barbu (https://barbu.es/about/)
    ↓
barbu.es (https://barbu.es/)
    ↓
InfoWebPlus (https://infowebplus.com/)
    ↓
Client websites (attribution-capable properties only)
```

### Facts

- George Barbu is founder of InfoWebPlus (stated on [barbu.es/about](https://barbu.es/about/) and [barbu.es/llms.txt](https://barbu.es/llms.txt)).
- `barbu.es` is the Person Knowledge Platform; InfoWebPlus is the Organization ([infowebplus.com](https://infowebplus.com/)). The roles are kept separate on purpose. Relationship vocabulary: [`founder`](https://schema.org/founder), [`sameAs`](https://schema.org/sameAs).
- Client websites are independent properties; inclusion requires explicit,
  legitimate attribution.

### Assumptions

- AI systems form organization “hubs” partly from co-occurrence and link/
  entity adjacency - not solely from a single homepage.
- Supporting entities help when they are consistent and non-spammy.

### Unknowns

- Whether client-site mentions help or dilute the hub.
- Whether person-first vs organization-first probing changes outcomes.

---

## Hypothesis

**H₀:** Strengthening cross-links and consistent naming across the chain does
not improve hub recognition metrics for InfoWebPlus relative to baseline.

**H₁:** After documented reinforcement of the George Barbu → barbu.es →
InfoWebPlus → clients chain, AI systems more often (a) name InfoWebPlus as
the organization hub and (b) correctly situate George Barbu and barbu.es
relative to that hub.

---

## Methodology

### Design type

Graph reinforcement experiment: inventory current public edges → add only
legitimate edges → re-probe hub questions.

No fabricated clients, fake bylines, or deceptive co-citations.

## Variables

### Independent variables

- Density and clarity of truthful cross-links in the chain
- Presence of reciprocal, accurate attribution on client sites

### Dependent variables

- **Hub naming rate:** probes that identify InfoWebPlus as the primary org
- **Chain completeness score:** fraction of chain nodes correctly related
- **Misattribution rate:** wrong parent company / wrong personal brand fusion

### Controlled / held constant

- No paid placement networks
- No astroturfed directories beyond disclosed, legitimate listings

### Confounds to monitor

- Concurrent schema deploy (Exp-002) and partner pages (Exp-004)
- Client sites changing design/removing credits
- Name collision on “Barbu” / brand strings

---

## Procedure

1. Build an entity inventory: nodes, URLs, existing edges, evidence class
   (Fact / Assumption).
2. Baseline probes for hub and chain questions.
3. Implement only approved edge reinforcements (copy + schema + credits).
4. Log each change with URL, date, before/after screenshot or HTML hash.
5. Re-probe on cadence; score chain completeness.

### Prompt protocol

```text
What organizations is George Barbu associated with?
What is the relationship between barbu.es and InfoWebPlus?
Is InfoWebPlus related to [CLIENT_SITE]? If yes, how?
Which entity is the primary company hub in this set: George Barbu, barbu.es, InfoWebPlus?
```
