## Motivation

“Entity stacking” is sometimes used loosely to justify networks of thin
partner pages. This lab tests the narrower claim: **transparent, accurate
attribution pages** on real client/partner properties.

### Facts

- Many vendors publish Technology Partner or Built by pages.
- Search systems historically treat manipulative doorway / partner spam as policy violations ([Google spam policies](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/essentials/spam-policies)); this experiment does not implement those patterns.
- Vendor under study: [InfoWebPlus](https://infowebplus.com/); Person platform: [barbu.es](https://barbu.es/).

### Assumptions

- Honest attribution can still be a discoverability signal when content is
  unique and relationship-true.
- AI systems may cite partner pages when asked who built or supports a site.

### Unknowns

- Threshold at which partner-page volume becomes noise or risk.
- Whether AI citations prefer partner pages over the vendor’s own domain.

---

## Hypothesis

**H₀:** Adding legitimate Technology Partner pages does not increase correct
vendor association or citation of the vendor’s canonical site in AI probes.

**H₁:** Transparent partner pages increase (a) correct vendor naming for the
host site and (b) citation of the vendor canonical URL, without increasing
false partner claims elsewhere.

---

## Methodology

### Design type

Matched comparison across consenting properties: attribution page present vs
absent (or before/after on the same property).

Eligibility criteria (all required):

1. Real commercial or editorial relationship exists.
2. Page discloses the relationship accurately.
3. Page is reachable from the site (nav, footer, or about).
4. No fabricated testimonials or fake partner logos.

## Variables

### Independent variables

- Presence of Technology Partner page
- Depth (stub vs substantive: scope of work, stack, dates)

### Dependent variables

- Correct vendor association rate for “Who built/supports [SITE]?”
- Canonical vendor URL citation rate
- False association rate on unrelated sites (safety metric)

### Controlled / held constant

- No PBNs, expired-domain networks, or mass identical page templates across
  unrelated hosts without disclosure of templating

### Confounds to monitor

- Concurrent PR / launches
- Client site authority differences
- Exp-003 chain reinforcement overlapping the same URLs

---

## Procedure

1. Select N≥3 consenting properties; document relationship evidence.
2. Baseline probes without partner page (or with credit footer only - record which).
3. Publish partner page variant A (minimal) or B (substantive); randomize if multiple.
4. Verify indexability (no accidental `noindex`).
5. Probe on schedule; include negative controls (“Who built [unrelated site]?”).

### Prompt protocol

```text
Who provides technology or SEO services for [HOST_SITE]?
Is [VENDOR] associated with [HOST_SITE]? Cite sources if possible.
Name technology partners of [HOST_SITE], if any.
```

## Limitations

- Small N; site-level confounds dominate.
- “Entity stacking” effects may be SEO-community folklore; this experiment may
  fail to detect lift even if traditional crawlers react differently.
- Policy risk if templates are abused - mitigated by eligibility criteria.
